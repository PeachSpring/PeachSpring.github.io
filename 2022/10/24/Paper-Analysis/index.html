Paper Analysis

2.Choose a few pages and note the section headings and the first sentences of each paragraph

  1. It has been shown by Bothe and others that beryllium when bombarded by α-particles of polonium emits a radiation of great penetrating power, which has been an absorption coefficient in lead of about 0.3 (cm)^-1.

  2. I have made some experiments using the valve counter to examine the properties of this radiation excited in beryllium.

  3. If we ascribe the ejection of the proton to a Compton recoil from a quantum of 52 x 10^6 electron volts, then the nitrogen recoil atom arising by a similar process should have an energy not greater than about 400,000 volts, should produce not more than about 10,000 ions, and have a range in air at N.T.P. of about 1.3 mm.

  4. These results, and others I have obtained in the course of the work, are very difficult to explain on the assumption that the radiation from beryllium is a quantum radiation, if energy and momentum are to be conserved in the collisions.

  5. If it be supposed that the radiation consists of quanta, then the capture of the α-particle by the Be^9 nucleus will form a C^13 nucleus.

  6. It is to be expected that many of the effects of a neutron in passing through matter should resemble those of a quantum of high energy, and it is not easy to reach the final decision between the two hypotheses.

3.Analyse the extent to which the paper can be read from the first sentences of each paragraph

  1. It gives the background and leads to the following. Maybe… nothing more.
  2. I guess Chadwick made some experiments about what mentioned above.
  3. The chances are that the ejection of the proton isn’t due to a Compton recoil.
  4. It seems that he had found something magnificent to overthrow traditional explanation.
  5. He is right?
  6. He is right.

4.Analyse the extent to which the paper followed the “rules of the paragraph” we have discussed

​ No equations, no tables, nice summary… This paper follows the “rules of the paragraph” well, it even won author the Nobel Prize.

5.Write a paragraph describing your opinion

​ I think it’s awesome for Chadwick to present a new concept about Neutron and prove it’s true against the background of some assumptions already made by authorities. I admire the Nobel Prize:)
